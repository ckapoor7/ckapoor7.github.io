Chaitanya Kapoor

I am a post-baccalaureate research assistant at UC San Diego, advised by Prof. Meenakshi Khosla. My work focuses on understanding the underlying neural representations of biological and artifical neural networks.

At present, I am also being mentored by Prof. Talmo Pereira at the Salk Institute of Biological Sciences, where I am working on the development of pose estimation methods for quantification of animal behavior.

I recently graduated from BITS Pilani, majoring in Electrical and Electronics Engineering. I was also a visiting student at the Senseable Intelligence Lab, under the supervision of Prof. Satrajit Ghosh, where I completed my undergraduate thesis.

In the past, I have been mentored by Prof. Ramesh Raskar at MIT Media Lab. I have also had the privilege of being a visiting student under the guidance of Prof. Donglai Wei and Prof. Ed Boyden at the McGovern Institute of Brain Research.

My research interests lie at the intersection of Deep Learning and Neuroscience. Through this lens, my aim is to design biologically-constrained neural networks by reverse engineering underlying neural computations in mammalian brains.
